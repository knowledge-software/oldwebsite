Free Fall
This is a simple example of graphing the motion of a free falling object. Each tick of the clock gives us a point on the graph to the right of the falling eight ball. The blue dots represent rhe posaition below the starting point with each tick. The red dots are the velocity of the "8" ball at that same instant of time. Notice how the points form a straight line. Finally the green dots along the bottom are the acceleration at each instant. Does the acceleration ever change? What kind of a relationship does the velocity have with time? Is it increasing or decreasing? Why is it negative?
