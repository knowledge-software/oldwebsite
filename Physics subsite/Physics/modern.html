"Modern Physics" is in quotes because this topic is nearly 100 years old. This unit is supposed to encompas the ideas of our physical world as they were expressed after the turn of the 20th Century. There is a common miscoception about the momentus events that lead to the modification of the existing physical models of the 19th Century. Physics did undergo a vast improvement but it wasn't overturned. The development of Quantum Mechanics and Relativity helped us understand the physical processes of the very small and cosmic scales respectively but classical mechanics is still valid for most of our experience. Classial rules still hold and, indeed, their principals are essential for the foundation of modern physics.

With quantum mechanics and relativity, however, some of our conventional, intuitive way at looking at the universe gets turned around. So, as Cypher said in the Matrix: "Buckle your seatbelt, Dorothy... 'cause Kansas is going bye-bye!"
