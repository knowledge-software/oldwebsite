Here are some of the customized menus I have created on my calculator that can speed up the process of solving various physics problems. Each menu assumes you have set up a corresponding directory, mechan for mechanics and electro for electricity and magnetism. This isn't essential, I just like it that way. As a result I just call the custom menu programs: menu(). What i have illustrated here are the program files as they should appear (more or less) in your calculator. You can't just cut and paste since the character codes for some of the more exotic characters are different on the ti89 and on this web page. You can just download the corresponding text files by clicking on the headers. These programs are just to get you started. You can modify them any way you wish once you load them into your calculator.

Mech

 
()
Prgm
setFold(mechan)
 Custom
  Title "Var"
    Item "m1"
    Item "m2"
    Item "T"
    Item "T1"
    Item "T2"
    Item "m3"
    Item "µ"
    Item "θ"
    Item "τ"
  Title "Kinetics"
    Item "h=1/2 _g t^2"
    Item "δx=v0 t+1/2 a t^2"
    Item "t=√(2 d/v)"
    Item "2a*d=vf^2-vi^2"
    Item "Iring=m*r^2"
    Item "Idisk=1/2*m*r^2"
    Item "Isph=2/5*m*r^2"
  Title "Solve"
    Item "solve("
    Item " and "
    Item "solve( and ,{a,T})"
    Item "zeros(,t)"
  Title "Unit"
    Item "_m"
    Item "_m/_s"
    Item "_m/_s^2"
    Item "_s"
    Item "_kg"
    Item "_N"
    Item "_J"
    Item "_W"
  Title "Const"
    Item "_g"
    Item "_Gc"
    Item "me=5.976E24*_kg"
    Item "ms=1.99E30*_kg"
  Title "Tool"
    Item "ClrHome"
    Item "NewProb"
    Item "main\menu()"
 EndCustm
 CustmOn
EndPrgm
